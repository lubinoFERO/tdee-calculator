How This Calculator Works

What is TDEE?

Your Total Daily Energy Expenditure is the total number of calories your body burns in a day, accounting for your activity level. It's the most important number for managing your weight.

What is BMR?

Basal Metabolic Rate is the number of calories your body needs at complete rest — just to keep your organs functioning. We calculate it using the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, the most scientifically validated formula.

Mifflin-St Jeor Equation

Men: BMR = (10 × kg) + (6.25 × cm) − (5 × age) + 5
Women: BMR = (10 × kg) + (6.25 × cm) − (5 × age) − 161

Your TDEE = BMR × activity multiplier.

How Are Macros Calculated?

Protein is set at 2g per kg of bodyweight for optimal muscle retention. Fat covers 25% of total calories. Remaining calories are allocated to carbohydrates. This is a performance-based macro split.

Frequently Asked Questions
How accurate is this TDEE calculator?
+
This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, which is considered the most accurate BMR formula for most people by registered dietitians. It's within ±10% for the majority of adults. For athletes or people with very high muscle mass, results may vary slightly.
How many calories should I eat to lose weight?
+
A safe and sustainable calorie deficit for weight loss is 500 kcal below your TDEE, which typically results in around 0.5kg (1lb) per week of fat loss. Deficits larger than 750 kcal/day can cause muscle loss and are not recommended long-term.
What activity level should I choose?
+
Choose "Sedentary" for desk jobs with no exercise. "Lightly Active" for 1-3 workouts per week. "Moderately Active" for 3-5 sessions. "Very Active" for daily intense training. Most people overestimate their activity level — when in doubt, go one level lower.
What is BMI and is it reliable?
+
BMI (Body Mass Index) is a simple height-to-weight ratio. It's useful as a general health screening tool but doesn't account for muscle mass. Athletes often have a high BMI despite being very lean. Use it as a reference, not as a definitive health metric.
How often should I recalculate my TDEE?
+
Recalculate every 4–6 weeks, or whenever your weight changes by more than 3–5kg. As you lose or gain weight, your BMR changes, so your calorie targets need to be updated for continued progress.
